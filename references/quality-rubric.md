# Quality Rubric

Use this rubric to review outputs and write `REVIEW_REPORT.md`. It does not replace human taste; it makes taste easier to discuss and improve.

Score out of 100:

| Category | Points | What Good Looks Like |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Narrative clarity | 10 | Viewer understands the promise, proof, and CTA quickly |
| Brand fidelity | 10 | Visual language fits the product and user-provided brand |
| Typography | 15 | Type scale, weight, spacing, and hierarchy feel intentional |
| Frame composition | 15 | Hero frames are balanced, readable, and uncluttered |
| Motion choreography | 15 | Motion orders attention and supports story beats |
| Transition quality | 10 | Transitions connect ideas and land cleanly |
| Audio sync | 10 | Visual hits align with music/narration where relevant |
| Technical stability | 10 | Deterministic, validated, no missing assets or runtime errors |
| Editability | 3 | Copy, color, timing, and scenes can be changed locally |
| Delivery readiness | 2 | Outputs, snapshots, and review notes are organized |

## Red Flags

- The first readable frame appears too late.
- The scene has more than one dominant message.
- Text fits desktop but not the intended platform crop.
- Every element uses the same entrance animation.
- The transition shows an empty or visually weak frame.
- CTA is smaller or weaker than decorative elements.
- Product proof is a claim without visual or factual support.
- The video depends on network assets at render time.
- A small user edit requires rewriting the whole composition.

## Review Method

Review in three passes:

1. Still-frame pass: inspect hero snapshots without playing the video.
2. Silent motion pass: play muted and check readability and rhythm.
3. Full pass: play with audio and check sync, emotional arc, and CTA.

If a video fails the still-frame pass, fix layout and typography before changing animation.

## Platform Notes

### YouTube 16:9

- Strong opening frame matters.
- Large type can carry the hook.
- Product proof can use a little more visual detail.
- CTA can hold longer.

### Shorts/Reels/TikTok 9:16

- Use larger safe margins for UI overlays.
- Reduce word count per beat.
- Keep central visual mass.
- Avoid tiny subtitles and wide horizontal layouts.

### Landing Page Hero Video

- Do not overcut.
- Avoid motion that distracts from adjacent page content.
- Loop points should be calm and seamless when looping is required.

## Feedback Translation

- "Looks cheap": reduce effects, improve typography, simplify palette, increase spacing.
- "Too slow": remove dead holds, sharpen transitions, shorten copy.
- "Too fast": reduce copy, increase hold time, simplify entrances.
- "Not Apple enough": more restraint, more negative space, fewer colors, slower precise reveals.
- "Doesn't sell": strengthen hook, proof, and CTA.
- "Feels like a template": add a product-specific visual moment or custom transition.
