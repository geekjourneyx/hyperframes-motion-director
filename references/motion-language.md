# Motion Language

Use motion as choreography, not decoration. A good ad tells the eye what to notice, when to read, and when to feel the transition.

## Timing Vocabulary

Use concrete timing instead of vague adjectives.

| Feel | Typical Duration | Ease | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Snappy | 0.25-0.45s | `power4.out`, `back.out(1.2)` | Social energy, quick product hits |
| Premium | 0.8-1.6s | `expo.out`, `sine.inOut` | Apple-like reveals, luxury pacing |
| Hype | 0.18-0.35s | `power3.out`, sharp scale | Bass hits, title impacts |
| Cinematic | 1.0-2.0s | `sine.inOut` | Camera drift, depth, slow product reveal |
| Technical | 0.3-0.8s | `steps`, `power2.out` | Typewriter, scan, grid, data reveal |

## Beat Structure

Most short ads need:

1. Hook: break the scroll or establish a strong promise.
2. Reveal: show product, offer, or concept.
3. Proof: a concrete feature, number, or visual evidence.
4. CTA: brand lockup and action.

Do not overload a beat. One beat, one idea.

## Hold Time

Important information needs time on screen.

- Big title: usually hold 0.6-1.2s after entering.
- Proof/stat: usually hold 0.8-1.5s.
- CTA: usually hold 1.0s or more.
- Very short flashes are for emphasis, not comprehension.

If viewers cannot read it in a silent preview, the motion is too fast or the copy is too dense.

## Entrance Patterns

Avoid making every element enter with `y + opacity`.

Mix patterns intentionally:

- Letter stagger for kinetic typography.
- Mask wipe for premium reveals.
- Scale lock for title impact.
- Clip-path reveal for product frames.
- Blur-to-sharp for depth.
- Grid scan for technical tone.
- Slow camera push for cinematic emphasis.
- Parallax layers for depth.

Use no more than one dominant entrance pattern per beat unless contrast is intentional.

## Transition Rules

For multi-scene videos, transitions carry the viewer from one idea to the next.

Good transitions:

- Match the energy of the next beat.
- Preserve direction or momentum.
- Hide layout replacement.
- Land on a readable hero frame.

Avoid:

- Fading every scene to empty before the transition.
- Cutting away before the viewer reads the point.
- Using a transition only because it looks flashy.
- Repeating the same transition every time unless it is a deliberate motif.

For HyperFrames compositions, let the transition handle the scene exit unless the final scene is ending the whole video.

## Audio Sync

Use audio hits for structural moments:

- Title impact.
- Product reveal.
- Proof/stat reveal.
- Scene transition.
- CTA lockup.

Use micro audio reactivity for small elements only: glow, slight scale, opacity, background texture. Do not let audio response control the main story unless it is deterministic and reviewable.

## Apple-Like Restraint

For Apple-like or premium launch videos:

- Use fewer elements.
- Use larger type.
- Increase negative space.
- Favor slow pushes and precise reveals.
- Avoid excessive particles, glitch, neon gradients, and random decorative motion.
- Let the product or phrase own the frame.

Premium usually comes from selection and timing, not from adding more effects.
